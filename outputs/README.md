# outputs : Generated Charts

This folder contains all visualizations generated by the source scripts.
Each chart directly supports a claim made in the solution and is referenced
in the PDF submission and Streamlit dashboard.

---

## Chart Reference

### from signal_validator.py

#### for_rider_delta_distribution.png
Distribution of time deltas between merchant FOR taps and rider GPS arrival
at the restaurant. The spike near zero seconds directly visualizes the
rider-influenced signal problem : merchants tapping Food Ready only when
the rider walks in, not when food is actually prepared.

Key insight : A large cluster of FOR signals falls within the 60-second
threshold, confirming systematic bias in the current labeling process.

---

#### label_quality_comparison.png
Side-by-side histogram comparing recorded KPT distributions for contaminated
labels vs clean labels, overlaid with the true KPT average.

Key insight : Contaminated labels are shifted away from true KPT, while
clean labels align closely with ground truth : proving that GPS-based
debiasing recovers label accuracy.

---

### from kitchen_load_score.py

#### kls_vs_true_kpt.png
Scatter plot of computed Kitchen Load Score (x-axis) against true KPT in
minutes (y-axis) for all orders in the clean dataset.

Key insight : Positive correlation of 0.561 confirms that the Kitchen Load
Score is a meaningful signal : higher kitchen stress genuinely predicts
longer preparation times.

---

#### kls_by_hour.png
Bar chart showing average Kitchen Load Score by hour of day across all
restaurants.

Key insight : Peak lunch hours (12 to 14) and dinner hours (19 to 21) show
clearly elevated kitchen load scores, validating that the score captures
real demand patterns.

---

#### kls_by_restaurant_size.png
Overlapping histogram showing Kitchen Load Score distribution broken down
by restaurant size : small, medium, and large.

Key insight : Larger restaurants consistently operate at higher kitchen load,
confirming that the score scales appropriately across different merchant types.

---

### from external_kitchen_rush.py

#### kitchen_load_breakdown.png
Stacked bar chart showing average hourly order volume broken down into three
sources : Zomato orders (visible), dine-in orders (invisible), and competitor
platform orders (invisible).

Key insight : Zomato sees only the bottom portion of total kitchen demand.
The majority of kitchen load during peak hours comes from sources the current
system cannot observe.

---

#### kpt_underestimation_by_hour.png
Line chart comparing true KPT (accounting for full kitchen load) against
Zomato-only KPT estimate by hour of day, with the underestimation gap shaded.

Key insight : The gap widens significantly during peak hours, reaching 13.99
minutes at lunch and dinner rush : directly causing early rider dispatch and
idle waiting at restaurants.

---

#### underestimation_by_size.png
Bar chart showing average KPT underestimation broken down by restaurant size.

Key insight : Larger restaurants suffer greater underestimation because they
handle more external orders, making the invisible load problem more severe
at high-volume partners.

---

### from kpt_model.py

#### mae_comparison.png
Side-by-side bar chart comparing Mean Absolute Error of the baseline model
(trained on dirty labels, no Kitchen Load Score) against the enriched model
(trained on clean labels with Kitchen Load Score as a feature).

Key insight : 33.2% reduction in MAE from 5.11 minutes down to 3.42 minutes,
directly demonstrating the business value of signal enrichment.

---

#### feature_importance.png
Horizontal bar chart showing feature importance scores from the enriched
LightGBM model.

Key insight : Kitchen Load Score and concurrent orders rank among the top
features, validating that the new signals meaningfully contribute to
prediction accuracy.

---

#### predicted_vs_actual.png
Scatter plot of enriched model predictions against true KPT values on the
held-out test set, with a perfect prediction reference line.

Key insight : Predictions cluster tightly around the reference line,
confirming that the enriched model generalizes well to unseen orders.

---

### from iot_simulator.py

#### iot_label_quality.png
Line chart comparing IoT sensor KPT, merchant FOR KPT, and true KPT across
200 simulated orders.

Key insight : IoT sensor KPT tracks true KPT almost perfectly, while merchant
FOR KPT deviates significantly for contaminated orders : confirming that
hardware verification eliminates label bias entirely.

---

#### iot_error_distribution.png
Overlapping histogram comparing absolute label errors for merchant FOR signals
vs IoT sensor signals, both measured against true KPT.

Key insight : IoT sensor errors are concentrated at zero while merchant FOR
errors are spread across a wide range, demonstrating the label quality
superiority of hardware-verified signals.
